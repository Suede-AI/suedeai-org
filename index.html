Agentic commerce

Programmable rights need a programmable payment layer.

Proof-of-creation, programmable IP, and provenance make rights legible to software. That only matters at the moment of use if an agent can also pay for the right when it consumes it. The Agent Commerce Protocol (ACP) describes what a hireable agent can do. x402 is the HTTP-native settlement layer that lets any client pay per call in stablecoins, without an account, without an API key, and without a human in the loop.

Suede Labs runs this layer in production today. 17 live x402 paid endpoints on Base mainnet cover music generation, video generation, voice, rights lookup, licensing routes, and provenance reads — each priced per call in USDC and discoverable through standard well-known files. Producer by Suede Labs is registered as a hireable Virtuals ACP agent for music and video generation and ACP/x402 consulting (ID 019e3991-374d-75f3-a6b8-17ff309b4cd2).

The category claim is straightforward: rights-aware atomic payment is a layer, and Suede is operating it. Discovery surfaces: x402.json, agent-card.json, agentic-commerce.json. Read more at /agentic-commerce/.
