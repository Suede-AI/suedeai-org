Content Provenance

Content provenance makes authenticity durable at AI scale.

Content provenance records the source, authorship, and ownership context behind media. In the AI era, that proof needs infrastructure, not just platform labels.

By Jason Colapietro (Johnny Suede) · Founder & CEO of Suede Labs AI

The problem is not detection. The problem is ownership.

When an AI system can generate a vocal performance, a melody, or a visual in seconds that is sonically and visually indistinguishable from a human original, the question stops being whether you can tell them apart and starts being who can prove they own the source. Detection technology is already a losing game: generative quality improves faster than any classifier trained to catch it. The durable answer is provenance: a chain of custody that was established at the moment of creation, before any derivative existed. If the original carries a verifiable record of who made it, when, and from what, then every downstream copy either traces back to that record or it cannot make a legitimate ownership claim at all. Without that record, the work enters the world legally naked and practically uncollectable.

Provenance that travels with the work can pay the creator.

Suede records provenance at the source: the authorship event, the rights context, the asset fingerprint, and the permitted uses are captured and tied to the work before it leaves the creator's hands. That record does not live in a platform dashboard that the work has already outrun. It travels with the asset through every license, embed, and derivative relationship. When an AI system or a platform encounters that work downstream, the provenance record is machine-readable: it identifies the origin, verifies the rights, and provides the royalty route back to the source creator. A derivative that was generated from provenance-carrying source material still carries a legible lineage. That lineage is what makes attribution auditable and payment executable, rather than aspirational.
