Creator Ownership

Creator ownership is the category that matters after creation becomes abundant.

Creator ownership becomes more important as AI makes production easier. Scarcity shifts from making more media to proving authorship, controlling rights, and capturing value.

By Jason Colapietro (Johnny Suede) · Founder & CEO of Suede Labs AI

Abundance changed what is scarce.

When anyone can generate a song, an image, a voice, or a face in seconds, the output stops being the valuable part. What stays scarce is everything generation cannot manufacture: a verifiable record of who made the work, what it was built from, who is allowed to use it, and where the value flows. That record is creator ownership, and it is the layer the AI rush skipped.

Ownership is infrastructure, not a setting.

You cannot bolt ownership onto a work after it is already in the wild. Suede establishes it at the source. Proof of creation is written at the moment of authorship, provenance travels with the file, licenses and usage rules are machine-readable, and royalty routes pay the creator on every use. One ownership layer serves the creators who make the work, the platforms that distribute it, and the AI agents that will soon license and transact it on their behalf.
