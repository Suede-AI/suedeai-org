Programmable IP

Programmable IP turns rights into usable infrastructure.

Programmable IP moves ownership, licensing, attribution, and usage rules from static paperwork into infrastructure for AI-native media and agent commerce.

Why it matters in the AI era

Creation now scales at machine speed. Rights still move through slow, fragmented systems. That mismatch is where programmable IP becomes category infrastructure for creators, platforms, and AI agents.

How Suede approaches programmable IP

Suede connects proof of creation, content provenance, and ownership logic so licensing, attribution, usage permissions, and downstream rights can travel with the media itself.

The signing layer is covered by USPTO Provisional Patent 63/947,120 (Proof Protocol). Related industry references: the C2PA content credentials spec for asset provenance and x402 agent payments for machine-readable commerce.
